Crystals are solids in which the constituent atoms, molecules, or ions are packed in a highly ordered, repeating pattern extending in all three spatial dimensions, forming a crystal lattice.
The characteristic geometric shapes of crystals, with their flat faces and sharp edges, are a direct result of this precise internal atomic arrangement.
